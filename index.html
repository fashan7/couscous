The Problem

Import Reliance & The Consumer Trust Crisis

South Asia pays European prices for a grain it cannot grow — and its consumers no longer trust the staple they've eaten for generations.

  • 100% of couscous consumed in Sri Lanka and the Maldives is imported from Europe. Branded products like San Remo retail at ~LKR 3,000/kg — structurally excluding mid-market consumers and compressing hospitality margins.
  • A high-profile food safety scandal involving artificially coloured and over-polished rice has fundamentally eroded consumer confidence in Sri Lanka's core daily staple. Millions of households are actively seeking a clean, traceable alternative grain.
  • The Maldives resort sector imports 100% of its food supply at premium logistics costs. Our Sri Lanka location offers a 420km sea route — a fraction of European supplier freight distance — translating directly into lower landed cost for resort procurement.
  • No domestic production infrastructure exists across the entire South Asian region. This is not competitive entry — it is category creation.

The Solution

First Domestic Automated Production Line

Build the only automated couscous manufacturing facility in South Asia — import grain in bulk, add value locally, and sell at a price no imported brand can match.

  • We procure extrusion and drying machinery directly from Chinese manufacturers — the same technology base powering European production — at a fraction of European capital cost. The process is commercially proven.
  • Structural arbitrage: importing bulk semolina (lower tariff) and processing it domestically grants local value-add status under Sri Lanka's duty framework — a legal, permanent, defensible cost advantage baked into regulation.
  • The facility is phased: starting with the Maldives B2B market and Colombo premium retail, then expanding as contracts are secured. Capital follows signed agreements, not projections.
  • Operationally simple — semolina, water, extrusion, controlled drying. No cold chain dependency, 18–24 month shelf life, sub-2% wastage. Focused, high-margin grain processing.

Product

The Clean, Fast Grain

100% pure durum wheat, zero additives, ready in 5 minutes. Built for the health-conscious consumer who has run out of reasons to trust their plate.

  • Pure durum wheat semolina, no artificial colouring, zero undisclosed additives. In a market where the rice supply chain has been publicly compromised, clean label is not a premium feature — it is a baseline requirement we meet from day one.
  • Five-minute steam cooking makes couscous the fastest complete grain meal in the Sri Lankan market — directly addressing the urbanised, time-constrained household and the fitness community.
  • Brand architecture built for trust: simple packaging, full ingredient transparency, and a clear origin story. Locally made, internationally sourced grain — 40–60% below comparable imported brands at retail.
  • Naturally high-protein, low-GI carbohydrate profile positions the product across health food, meal-prep staple, and everyday grain alternative — broadening shelf placement and basket penetration.

Market Opportunity

Maldives B2B Anchor & Regional Scale

We are not entering a market — we are building one. The immediate addressable revenue sits 420km off our coastline.

  • The Maldives hosts 170+ luxury resort properties serving 1.8M+ international tourists annually, importing virtually every food input. Our 420km sea route delivers faster replenishment at materially lower freight cost than any European competitor.
  • Sri Lanka's premium grocery segment (Keells, Cargills, Arpico) serves an urban middle class spending LKR 18,000–35,000/household/month on food. Even 1% penetration generates meaningful initial domestic revenue.
  • Conservative Phase 1 model: 500 MT annual production capacity at blended LKR 800/kg across B2B and retail yields LKR 400M top-line — requiring less than 0.1% combined market penetration before any export scale-up.
  • Broader TAM: Indian Ocean island economies (Seychelles, Mauritius, Réunion) and GCC South Asian expatriate consumer base — all high-import-cost markets with established couscous consumption and no regional supplier.

Technology Moat

In-House Proprietary ERP via AI / Vibe Coding

Most food manufacturers our size pay LKR 5M–15M to software vendors for systems they do not own. We build and own ours — and that cost advantage compounds every year we operate.

  • The founder is a production-grade software engineer building our entire internal tech stack — ERP, inventory, logistics, and B2B portal — in-house using AI-assisted development, at effectively zero licensing cost, eliminating a recurring overhead that cripples most SME manufacturers in Sri Lanka.
  • Our distributor dashboard and B2B client portal give resort partners real-time visibility into order status, delivery ETAs, and reorder thresholds. In a market where most local manufacturers operate on WhatsApp and spreadsheets, this positions us as a credible, modern supply partner.
  • The proprietary stack creates genuine switching costs. When a Maldives procurement manager can view usage analytics and set automated reorder triggers, they are not just buying couscous — they are integrating into a supply system. That stickiness does not appear on the balance sheet.

  • Imported finished couscous retails at ~LKR 3,000/kg inclusive of finished-goods import duty, freight, importer margin, distributor margin, and retailer margin.
